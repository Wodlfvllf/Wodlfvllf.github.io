Research

Research interests

I am interested in the boundary between learning algorithms and the systems required to study them rigorously. Three questions currently organize my work.

01

Post-training and evaluation

How can reward design, advantage estimation, clipping, and regularization be compared without coupling each algorithmic idea to a specific trainer?

02

Scalable learning systems

How should parallelism, communication, memory, and rollout infrastructure be organized for large language, multimodal, and mixture-of-experts models?

03

Reliable applied learning

How can models improve on ambiguous, high-stakes data while satisfying latency, verification, and deployment constraints?

Research softwareRL post-training

grpo-composer

A modular experimentation layer on top of veRL for GRPO-style post-training. It separates reward functions, advantage estimators, clipping strategies, regularizers, aggregation schemes, and reward transforms from the trainer core, making algorithmic comparisons easier to implement and audit.

veRL · PyTorch · GRPO · Distributed rollout and training

SystemsDistributed training

QuintNet

A PyTorch framework that studies five dimensions of model parallelism: data, tensor, pipeline, sequence, and expert. The implementation targets GPT-style language models, vision-language models, and mixture-of-experts architectures, with emphasis on model partitioning and communication-aware training.

PyTorch · NCCL · DDP/FSDP · 5D parallelism

Scientific MLML4SCI at CERN

Self-supervised learning for CMS detector images

An end-to-end pipeline for 3.8M+ detector images from the CMS experiment. A masked autoencoder with a Vision Transformer reached 98.3% AUC with a 99.9% parameter reduction. Pretraining was scaled across A100 GPUs with FSDP and released as a reproducible scientific ML workflow.

Masked autoencoders · ViT · FSDP · A100 GPUs

Dual-agent self-play with GRPO

A system in which one Qwen3-4B agent generates logic-based multiple-choice questions and a second agent solves them through reasoning. The training setup combines task-specific reward shaping, KL regularization, GRPO, and self-play with dynamic difficulty.
